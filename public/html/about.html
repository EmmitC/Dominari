Our Story

Founded in 2019 by a collective of literary enthusiasts, editors, and industry veterans, Dominari Publishing emerged from a simple yet profound belief: exceptional stories deserve exceptional care.

We witnessed too many remarkable manuscripts lost in the noise of mass publishing, too many readers struggling to discover their next literary obsession, and too many authors feeling disconnected from their audience.

Dominari was born to bridge these gaps—creating a publishing house that operates more like a literary curator, where every book is chosen not just for its commercial potential, but for its ability to move, challenge, and transform readers.

"We don't just publish books; we cultivate literary experiences that create lasting connections between stories and souls."

Senior Director & Head Of Legal Affairs

Hood Lubowa is a Ugandan lawyer, author(having written the novel Out of Dust.), human rights advocate, and the Civic Engagement and Digital Rights Lead for Oxfam in Uganda. Since January 2023, he has served as the Civic Engagement and Digital Rights Lead at Oxfam, based in Uganda.
He is actively involved in discussions and initiatives concerning digital rights, particularly in the Ugandan context, and has been a panelist at events on the subject. In his novel Out of Dust, he explores themes of loss, violence, and intolerance through the story of the character Suru. He holds a Bachelor of Laws (LLB) from Uganda Christian University and has been cited as a source on legal matters related to intellectual property.
